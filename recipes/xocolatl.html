Xocolatl

Description

Dating to antiquity, xocolatl was first developed by indigenous peoples of Mesoamerica, and one of the key early manuscript sources for this cacao drink is the Florentine Codex, originally titled La Historia General de las Cosas de Nueva España, which was compiled in sixteenth-century Mexico City. This encyclopaedic manuscript provides a record of Nahua culture and life, including details about the preparation and medicinal properties of age-old cacao-based decoctions. The Franciscan friar Bernadino de Sahagún is often credited as the author, but the work was evidently produced in partnership with Nahua people and is, in fact, written in both Nahuatl and Spanish. The following recipe is inspired by accounts of xocolatl in the Florentine Codex, with measurements left to the mixologist's discretion.

Ingredients

  • cacao beans
  • dried maize kernels (optional emulsifier)
  • water
  • achiote or annatto seeds
  • traditional flavourings of choice:
    • hueinacaztli (peppery and spicy)
    • izquixochitl (notes of rose)
    • mecaxochitl (notes of anise)
    • tlilxochitl or vanilla bean

Method

  1. Process cacao beans by grinding and mixing the grounds with a small amount of water.
  2. Pour mixture back and forth between two cups to aerate.
  3. Remove the foam and allow remaining product to thicken and harden.
  4. If using, grind dried maize kernels.
  5. Mix processed cacao with ground maize and a small amount of water.
  6. Add achiote for colour and flavourings to taste.
  7. Drink chilled.
