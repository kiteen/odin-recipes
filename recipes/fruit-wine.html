Fruit Wine

Description

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds of berries, such as:
    • Gooseberries
    • Currants
    • Raspberries
    • Cherries
  • 1 quart of boiled water
  • 1 pound of sugar

Method

  1. Bruise the fruits and steep them for 24 hours in water, stirring intermittently.
  2. Strain the liquid through "a hayre sive" to remove the pulp.
  3. Add the sugar to the clear liquid and stir to dissolve.
  4. Pour the mixture into a "earthen pott" with a stopper, closing tightly, and let stand "for a fortnight or 3 weeks."
  5. After the specified time has elapsed, bottle the wine and after 3 months, "it will be ready to drinke and keepe [for] 12 months."

Notes

hayre sive
A hayre sive or hair sieve is a fine sieve made of haircloth, that is, the hair of horses or camels. A modern equivalent might be a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
